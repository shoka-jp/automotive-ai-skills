# Toyota 2AZ-FE — Excessive oil consumption

**English** | [日本語](./toyota-2az-fe-oil-consumption.ja.md)

## Scope

- **Vehicles:** Toyota Camry, RAV4, Scion tC/xB, and other 2AZ-FE-powered models — most affected are roughly 2007–2011 model years (2AZ-FE was used more widely; the consumption problem concentrates in this window)
- **Engines/components:** 2AZ-FE 2.4L four — pistons and low-tension oil control rings
- **Production window:** Approximately 2006–2011 production
- **Markets:** Global engine; the formal warranty enhancement program (ZE7) was a US program — other markets handled cases individually

## The issue

The piston oil control ring design allows rings to stick and clog with carbon, letting the engine burn oil at rates far beyond normal. Consumption can reach a liter per 1,000–2,000 km in affected engines. Left unmonitored, low oil can progress to bearing and catalytic converter damage.

## Symptoms an owner can detect

- Oil level noticeably dropping between changes with no visible leaks
- Low-oil pressure light or low-level warning appearing mid-interval
- Blue-tinged exhaust smoke under acceleration after long idling (advanced cases)
- Fouled spark plugs / misfire codes in advanced cases

## Severity

**Major.** The engine rarely fails suddenly, but the proper fix is expensive, and unmonitored oil loss can destroy the engine and catalyst.

## Frequency (honest assessment)

Common enough that Toyota ran a formal US warranty enhancement (piston replacement) — but many engines never develop meaningful consumption, and consistent maintenance appears to reduce risk. Verify each individual car by measurement, not reputation.

## Fix and cost

- **Repair:** Proper fix is replacement pistons and rings (updated design) — an engine-out-scale job. Band-aids: more frequent oil changes, thicker oil, piston-soak cleaning treatments (mixed results)
- **Typical cost:** USD 2,000–4,500 / JPY 30–60万円 at independent shops for the piston job (US/Japan estimates); many owners instead monitor and top up

## Buyer checklist

- Ask directly about oil consumption and top-up habits; check for top-up bottles in the trunk
- Check service records for the piston/ring repair or (US) ZE7 program work — a fixed engine is a plus, not a minus
- On the test drive: watch for smoke on hard acceleration after a long idle
- Buy with the assumption you'll run an oil consumption test (measure over 1,000 km) in the first month

## Sources

Toyota US Warranty Enhancement Program ZE7 and related TSBs; extensively documented in Toyota/Scion owner communities and independent shop experience. Verify applicability by VIN and market.
