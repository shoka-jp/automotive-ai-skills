# Subaru EJ25 (SOHC) — External head gasket leaks

**English** | [日本語](./subaru-ej25-head-gasket.ja.md)

## Scope

- **Vehicles:** Subaru Legacy/Outback, Forester, Impreza (non-turbo) with the SOHC EJ25 — roughly 1999–2011 depending on model
- **Engines/components:** EJ251/EJ252/EJ253 2.5L SOHC flat-four — composite head gaskets at the head-to-block joint (the earlier DOHC EJ25D, ~1996–1999, had a different *internal* leak pattern)
- **Production window:** Late 1990s through late 2000s; later FB-series engines are a different design
- **Markets:** Global; especially well documented in North America where these models sold in volume

## The issue

The composite head gaskets weep externally at the head-block seam — typically oil on the left (driver's-side US) bank and coolant on the right — long before any dramatic failure. Slow coolant loss can progress to overheating, and the boxer layout means the seep is easy to miss until it drips.

## Symptoms an owner can detect

- Oil or coolant weep/crust along the head-block seam, visible from under the car at the bottom of each cylinder head
- Slow coolant level drop with no visible puddle; sweet smell after hot shutoff
- In progressed cases: temperature creep on hills or in traffic, bubbles in the overflow tank

## Severity

**Major.** The leak itself starts as an annoyance, but ignored coolant loss becomes overheating, and overheating kills these engines.

## Frequency (honest assessment)

Genuinely common at high mileage — a defining trait of the SOHC EJ25 era. Many owners run years with a minor weep and vigilant coolant checks; the risk is neglect, not suddenness.

## Fix and cost

- **Repair:** Head gaskets both sides with machined/checked heads and updated (typically MLS) gaskets; timing belt, water pump, and seals done in the same job because the labor overlaps almost entirely
- **Typical cost:** USD 1,800–3,000 / JPY 25–45万円 at independent Subaru specialists, including the timing-belt-service overlap

## Buyer checklist

- Flashlight inspection along both head-block seams from below — crusty trails are the tell
- Ask whether head gaskets were ever done, with which gasket type, and whether the heads were machined
- Check coolant condition and level, and the overflow tank for oil film
- Price an unfixed high-mileage example as if the job is due, because it likely is

## Sources

Ubiquitous in Subaru owner communities and independent Subaru specialist documentation across two decades. Details vary by year and market — verify against the specific engine code.
