# BMW N20/N26 — Timing chain and oil pump drive chain guide failure

**English** | [日本語](./bmw-n20-timing-chain.ja.md)

## Scope

- **Vehicles:** BMW F30 320i/328i, F10 528i, E84 X1 28i, F25 X3 28i and other N20/N26-powered models
- **Engines/components:** N20B20 and N26B20 2.0L turbo four — timing chain guides and oil pump drive chain module
- **Production window:** Engines built roughly 2011 to early 2015; BMW revised the chain module around 01/2015 (verify an individual car by production date and any documented repair)
- **Markets:** Global; a US class-action settlement extended coverage for some owners — other markets vary

## The issue

The plastic timing chain guides and the oil pump drive chain module can wear and break well before the engine's expected life. Fragments and a slack or derailed chain can cause loss of oil pressure or valve-to-piston contact — either outcome is typically a complete engine failure.

## Symptoms an owner can detect

- **Cold-start rattle** for 1–3 seconds from the rear/top of the engine — the classic early sign; insist on a stone-cold start when inspecting
- Whining or ticking that follows engine RPM once wear progresses
- Low oil pressure warning (late stage — stop driving immediately)
- Debris in the oil filter housing or pan (visible at oil changes)

## Severity

**Critical.** Failure while driving usually destroys the engine; repair at that point costs more than many affected cars are worth.

## Frequency (honest assessment)

Common enough to be the defining known issue of early N20s, but not universal — many pre-2015 engines with good oil-change history have survived past 150,000 km. Risk rises with long oil-change intervals and unknown history.

## Fix and cost

- **Repair:** Preventive replacement of the timing chain, guides, oil pump drive chain module, and related tensioners — proper kits address all of it in one job
- **Typical cost:** Roughly USD 1,500–3,000 / JPY 20–40万円 at an independent shop (US/Japan estimates; parts plus significant labor). Catastrophic failure: engine replacement, usually uneconomical

## Buyer checklist

- Ask specifically whether the chain module was ever replaced, and demand the invoice
- Cold-start the car yourself; walk away from startup rattle unless priced as needing the job
- Check the production date (driver's door jamb) against the ~2015 revision
- Budget the preventive job into your offer on any unverified pre-2015 example

## Sources

Widely documented in BMW owner communities and independent BMW specialist shops; US class-action settlement (Bang v. BMW of North America) extended warranty coverage for certain models. Verify current applicability for your market and VIN.
